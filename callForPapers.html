Submission Guidelines

Subject of Email:

In the subject of the email, mention "Technical/Medical Paper Submission."

Abstract Format:

  • Submit the abstract in Microsoft Word format.
  • The abstract should be approximately 500-750 words in length.
  • Structure the abstract with the following sections: background, aims, methods, results, and conclusion.

Text Formatting:

  • Use Times New Roman font, size 12.
  • Maintain double spacing throughout the abstract, including headers and footnotes.
  • Set 1-inch (2.5 cm) margins on all sides of the document.

Tables and Diagrams:

  • Properly number all tables and diagrams.
  • Include tables and diagrams at the end of the paper.

Plagiarism Check:

  • Internal students must submit a plagiarism check report along with the abstract.
  • Other students, the plagiarism percentage must be less than 10% will be considered.

Author Information:

  • Include author titles (such as Mr., Ms., Dr.).
  • Provide the names of all authors.
  • Mention the affiliation of the corresponding author.
  • Include the email address of the corresponding author within the paper.

Submission Deadline:

Submit either the full paper or the abstract to the email address smuresearchColloquium23@gmail.com. Ensure the submission is sent no later than 1st August 2023.

Poster Preparation Guidelines

Poster Display:

  • The poster display area will be limited to one side of a 4-foot by 8-foot tack board.
  • Recommended Poster Size: 4 feet by 4 feet.

Content Information:

  • Include the abstract title, author and co-author names, and the institution(s) where the research is underway.

Abstract Reproduction:

  • Place multiple copies of a reproduction of the abstract in the upper left-hand side of the poster.
  • Use the headings "Introduction/Background," "Methods," "Results," and "Conclusions" to structure the content.

Contact Information:

Include your contact information on the copies of the abstract. This will allow attendees who desire further information to easily reach out to you.

Awards

Our aim is to provide the delegates a unique platform to exhibit their work, interact, and learn from one another. The best entries in each category will be selected either to present their paper at the podium or as a poster, which will be intimated in the acceptance letter. The best poster and paper presentation will be awarded.

Best Paper Award:

Best paper or poster award in each category will be announced at the end of the Colloquium and a cash prize and certificate will be issued to the author. Certificates will also be issued to the authors who present their papers in the Colloquium.
